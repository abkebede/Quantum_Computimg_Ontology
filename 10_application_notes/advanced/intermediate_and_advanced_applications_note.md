# Intermediate and Advanced Applications Note

The intermediate and advanced examples connect quantum circuits to broader problem families.

## Optimization

QAOA and MaxCut are entry points into optimization problems such as scheduling, network partitioning, logistics, and resource allocation.

## Chemistry and materials

Hamiltonians written as sums of Pauli operators are the bridge from quantum code to molecular energy, catalysis, correlated materials, spin chains, and condensed-matter models.

## Signal, phase, and spectral problems

QFT and phase estimation connect quantum circuits to period finding, eigenvalue estimation, spectroscopy-like problems, and quantum simulation.

## Machine learning

Quantum kernels encode classical data into quantum states and compare data through state overlap. This is a route into quantum machine learning.

## Hardware realism

Noise comparison and readout mitigation show why real hardware differs from ideal simulation and why error characterization is part of practical quantum computing.
