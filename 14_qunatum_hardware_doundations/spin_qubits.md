# Spin Qubits

Spin qubits use electron or nuclear spin states in semiconductors, defects, or molecular systems.

## Physical basis

The computational basis is formed from two spin states, often represented as spin-up and spin-down.

## Control

Magnetic resonance, electric-dipole spin resonance, microwave pulses, or gate voltages control the spin state.

## Measurement

Measurement can use spin-to-charge conversion, optical readout, or magnetic resonance methods.

## Strengths

- Small physical size
- Potential compatibility with semiconductor fabrication
- Long coherence possible, especially for nuclear spins

## Challenges

- Precise materials control is critical
- Charge noise, hyperfine noise, and interface defects can limit performance
- Scaling and uniformity are difficult
