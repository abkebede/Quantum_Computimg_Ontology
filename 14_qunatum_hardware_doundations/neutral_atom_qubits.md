# Neutral-Atom Qubits

Neutral-atom qubits use atoms trapped by optical tweezers or optical lattices.

## Physical basis

Qubit states are usually hyperfine ground states or states coupled through Rydberg excitation.

## Control

Laser pulses manipulate single atoms. Rydberg interactions provide strong controllable two-qubit interactions.

## Measurement

Optical imaging detects atom presence and state-dependent fluorescence.

## Strengths

- Large arrays are possible
- Reconfigurable geometry
- Strong Rydberg-mediated interactions

## Challenges

- Laser stability and atom loss
- Gate fidelity and crosstalk
- Measurement and reset logistics at scale
