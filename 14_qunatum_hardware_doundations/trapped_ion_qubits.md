# Trapped-Ion Qubits

Trapped-ion qubits use individual atomic ions held by electromagnetic fields in ultra-high vacuum.

## Physical basis

The qubit is encoded in internal electronic or hyperfine states of an ion.

## Control

Laser or microwave pulses implement single-qubit gates. Shared motional modes or engineered interactions implement entangling gates.

## Measurement

State-dependent fluorescence converts the ion state into an optical signal.

## Strengths

- Long coherence times
- High-fidelity gates
- Natural all-to-all connectivity in small chains

## Challenges

- Slower gates compared with superconducting qubits
- Complex laser, vacuum, and trapping systems
- Scaling requires ion shuttling, modular traps, or photonic interconnects
