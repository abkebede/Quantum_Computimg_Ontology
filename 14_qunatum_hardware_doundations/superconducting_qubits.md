# Superconducting Qubits

Superconducting qubits are lithographically fabricated circuits operating at millikelvin temperatures. The most common modern form is the transmon qubit.

## Physical basis

The qubit is formed from quantized energy levels of a nonlinear superconducting circuit. The Josephson junction supplies the nonlinearity.

## Control

Single-qubit gates are performed with microwave pulses. Two-qubit gates use controlled coupling, resonator-mediated interactions, or tunable elements.

## Measurement

Measurement usually uses microwave readout through a resonator. The resonator response depends on the qubit state.

## Strengths

- Fast gates
- Chip-based fabrication
- Compatible with microwave electronics
- Large ecosystem of control hardware and software

## Challenges

- Requires dilution refrigeration
- Decoherence from loss, noise, materials defects, and crosstalk
- Scaling requires complex wiring and control electronics
